About Sign Language

Sign languages (also known as signed languages) are languages that use the visual-manual modality to convey meaning, instead of spoken words. Sign languages are expressed through manual articulation in combination with non-manual markers. Sign languages are full-fledged natural languages with their own grammar and lexicon. Sign languages are not universal and are usually not mutually intelligible, although there are also similarities among different sign languages.

Dumb and deaf people face great difficulty while interactingwith normal people. As the hearing impaired ordeaf people cannot talk like normal people, so they aredependent on a kind of visual communication most of thetimes. A sign language is a way of communication thatuses visual modality instead of acoustic sound patterns forexchanging information. People with hearing and speechimpairment use different sign and gestural languages intheir daily face-to-face communication.

There are differentmutually unintelligible sign languages around the world,because the languages were developed independently ofother deaf communities. American Sign Language, BritishSign Language, French Sign Language, Australian SignLanguage and many others have developed in various deafcommunities. Similarly, Pakistan Sign Language(PSL) isdeveloped in Pakistan by the deaf and dumb communitywhich has its own vocabulary and syntax.

Sign language is a way of communication among deaf communities. The sign language consists of dynamic or static hand configuration.

Sign alphabets are based on static hand poses which represent the letters of the alphabets and it uses gestures besides verbal communication. However, in the existing system, deaf people face a major problem to communicate with non-deaf

people through sign language because non-deaf people do not understand it. The advancement in computer vision enables us to produce some complex models that can recognize the sign, hand configuration, and translate it into text and voice. These models require a dataset to get trained. However, the dataset of Pakistan sign language (Urdu) is not available publicly.

Android App

Our mobile application is a dedicated effort to promote and preserve the beauty of Urdu Sign Language alphabets. With a focus on inclusivity, our app provides an accessible platform for individuals to learn and understand this unique form of communication. Through a user-friendly interface, users can explore the intricacies of Urdu Sign Language alphabets, fostering a deeper connection to this culturally rich expression. This initiative is an integral part of our broader project, aiming to bridge communication gaps and celebrate linguistic diversity. Join us in this journey as we empower individuals to communicate effectively through the universal language of signs.
